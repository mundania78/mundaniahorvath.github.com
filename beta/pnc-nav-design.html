PNC Navigation

Redesigning PNC's Navigation Experience

Offering a wider range of products and simultaneously expanding into other areas, PNC.com's navigation was an ambitious redesign.

Overview

PNC Financial Services is the 5th largest bank in the US. It provides visitors a variety of services such as, online banking, loans, customer service-related information, and educational content. To help make this information easier to find, regardless of someone’s familiarity with PNC’s website, my team and I worked on redesigning the main menu navigation

The Challenge

Due to the volume of content on PNC.com, the navigation started to become overwhelming to our visitors. At its core, this was a design challenge to optimize the information architecture. Our team needed to reimagine the visual and interaction design of the navigation to support a more user friendly and accessible presentation this involved.

First Step, Defining Taxonomy

The first step and a key goal was to define a new taxonomy that combined and eliminated certain categories for the new navigation. Behind the scenes there were small tests being done to the current menu to see if our audience preferred one term over another such as “borrow” versus “lend.”

In order to define our new categories, we did a series of card sorts. Participants were asked to sort products into groups and give each group a name. During this process, we realized that the current navigation had menu names that were not self-explanatory or duplicated items.

My team sorted through the results to find common names and came up with a proposed taxonomy for each section, which I reviewed with the stakeholders in our deparments.

Four Main Areas of PNC.com

The navigation was being treated as a marketing tool with the intent of driving more customers to PNC's financial products and services. One of the first things that I did was move our logo to the very top. I felt as though it was shoved under the first tier on the previous navigation. I increased the height of the tiers and font sizes.

Usability Testing Results

After agreeing on a rough design of the navigation, I built a prototype in Sketch and pulled it into InVision to make it interactive. The prototype was tested in different scenarios and tasks with users so I could make more informed design decisions.

Iterations

After the key takeaways from the Usability Test, I reworked a couple of the categories for the navigation. The example here focuses on the desktop experience because it is responsible for the majority of our visitors. Also, since PNC's main brand color orange was not ADA compliant, I had to rethink the color scheme for our links and titles.

Final Designs and Positive Results

This was the biggest menu update since 2014. Once the new navigation was launched, the number of categories viewed per session increased, and gateways recieved 35% more traffic. The categories gained higher ranks over the past year and more sub-categories were available to be indexed by search engines. We also received positive reactions and reviews from our business partners.

Key Takeaways

I am pleased with how the navigation turned out and how I was able to put a large amount of information into a fairly simple design. Here are a few takeaways from this project:

  • 1. Simplicity is key in designing a complex menu
  • 2. Seek out feedback early and continually from users and partners
  • 3. Run tests in the beginning of the process to get feedback earlier
